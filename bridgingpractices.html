Bridging Practices

Exploring the intersection of creativity and academia

My work as a graphic designer has taught me the power of visual communication. This skill informs my academic research and teaching, enabling me to create engaging presentations, visual aids, and interactive learning experiences.

Years of performing in DIY punk and indie bands taught me the value of collaboration and experimentation. These experiences shape my pedagogical methods, emphasizing teamwork, creative problem-solving, and adaptability.

My work in experimental media, such as creating Twine projects and interactive timelines, reflects my commitment to exploring new forms of storytelling. These projects bridge academic theory and creative practice.
